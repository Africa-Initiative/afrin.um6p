NextAfrica: Bridging Europe and Africa Through Innovation at Station F

February 28, 2025 Azeez Hamzat

Mohammed VI Polytechnic University (UM6P) has taken a significant step in its mission to foster innovation and entrepreneurship across Africa and beyond. On February 27, 2025, UM6P officially inaugurated its presence at Station F, the world’s largest startup campus in Paris, marking the launch of NextAfrica—a pioneering transcontinental acceleration program. Focused on Greentech, Agritech, and Healthtech, NextAfrica aims to bridge Europe and Africa by connecting startups, talents, investors, and industries to create sustainable, impactful solutions for the future.

A New Hub for Innovation at Station F

UM6P’s implantation at Station F, under the theme “Innovating Together: A Bridge Between Europe and Africa to Transform the Future,” positions the university as a strategic connector between the two continents. Station F, known for hosting over 1,000 startups annually, offers an unparalleled environment for innovation and collaboration. UM6P has secured an dedicated space with over 40 workstations, creating a hub where African and European startups can collaborate, innovate, and grow together.

NextAfrica is more than just an acceleration program—it’s a transformative platform that fosters collaboration, knowledge sharing, and co-creation. By combining the entrepreneurial ecosystem of Station F with UM6P’s cutting-edge infrastructure in Morocco, NextAfrica provides startups with a unique dual immersion experience: access to European markets and networks in Paris, and an African innovation hub via UM6P’s StartGate campus in Benguerir.

NextAfrica: Accelerating Greentech, Agritech, and Healthtech Startups

NextAfrica targets startups in Greentech, Agritech, and Healthtech—sectors critical to addressing Africa’s environmental, agricultural, and healthcare challenges. The program offers a six-month acceleration journey, during which selected startups benefit from:

  • Immersion at Station F: Access to a global network of entrepreneurs, investors, and mentors in Paris, one of the world’s leading innovation hubs.
  • Resources at UM6P: Utilization of UM6P’s advanced facilities, including labs and research expertise, through the StartGate campus in Morocco.
  • Mentorship and Connections: Support from a network of African and European mentors, as well as strategic connections with investors and industrial partners.

The program’s hybrid approach ensures that startups can develop, test, and structure their innovations while gaining insights into both European and African markets. Fahd Benkirane, Director General of UM6P France, emphasized the program’s vision: “NextAfrica reflects our ambition to create an ecosystem where startups from Africa and Europe collaborate, innovate, and grow together. Through our presence at Station F and UM6P’s deep roots in Africa, we offer entrepreneurs a unique framework to accelerate their development and tackle tomorrow’s technological and societal challenges.”

UM6P Ventures at Station F: Fueling Startup Growth

Alongside NextAfrica, UM6P Ventures, the investment arm of UM6P, has also established a presence at Station F. This move enhances access to funding for startups by creating synergies between European and African venture capital ecosystems. Yassine Laghzioui, Director of Entrepreneurship and Venturing at UM6P and CEO of UM6P Ventures, highlighted the impact: “By alternating between Station F and StartGate, startups gain a dual immersion—opening doors to Europe while staying anchored in an African innovation hub. Our goal is to validate their technology, accelerate their market entry, and secure the funding they need to scale rapidly.”

UM6P Ventures’ involvement ensures that startups not only receive mentorship and resources but also have opportunities to connect with investors, fostering a sustainable path to growth and impact.

Building Bridges for a Sustainable Future

NextAfrica positions UM6P as a key player in connecting talents, infrastructure, and capital to drive the next wave of African tech scale-ups. The program’s focus on Greentech, Agritech, and Healthtech aligns with UM6P’s broader mission to address Africa’s most pressing challenges through innovation. By fostering collaboration between African and European startups, NextAfrica creates a bridge for knowledge exchange, technology transfer, and co-creation, ensuring that solutions are both globally competitive and locally relevant.

The launch of NextAfrica also underscores UM6P’s growing international presence. With its roots in Benguerir, Morocco, and now an established foothold in Paris, UM6P is building a global network that empowers entrepreneurs to shape a sustainable future for Africa and beyond.
